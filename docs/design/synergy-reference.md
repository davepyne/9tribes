## 45 Pair Synergies

### Venom (V) Pairs

**V+F — Toxic Bulwark**
Fortress units radiate a poison zone: enemies within 1 hex of a fortress-tagged unit take 2 poison damage per turn (passive, stacks with venom on-hit). Defenders become the danger.

**V+S — Stampeding Death**
Elephant charges inflict double poison duration. Stampede knockback leaves enemies poisoned in their new position. A charging elephant through a line of enemies poisons everyone it hits.

**V+C — Viper Strike**
Cavalry with poison get guaranteed poison on charge attacks (100% proc vs normal chance). Charge poison deals 3× initial damage. Fast, deadly, gone before you notice.

**V+H — Poisoned Skirmish**
After retreating, the unit leaves a poison trap on the hex it vacated. Any enemy that moves into that hex takes poison damage and is slowed (1 less move next turn). Hit and fade, leave death behind.

**V+T — Venomous Tide**
Naval units with poison contaminate coastal hexes. Enemies on contaminated coast hexes take 2 poison damage/turn. Amphibious assaults from contaminated water deal bonus poison on landing.

**V+N — Regenerative Venom**
Poisoned enemies heal less (50% healing reduction). Nature healing units with venom apply a "withering" effect that prevents enemy regeneration. Anti-healer.

**V+J — Death from the Shadows**
Stealth ambushes with poison deal double damage AND apply 2 poison stacks instantly. The first strike from stealth is lethal. If the enemy survives, they're heavily poisoned and the attacker vanishes.

**V+P — Desert Viper**
Camel units with venom ignore terrain for *positioning* (already do) AND apply poison through difficult terrain. Moving through rough terrain doesn't slow them, and enemies in rough terrain near them take passive poison damage (heat + venom = no escape).

**V+V — Concentrated Venom**
(Having venom from multiple sources) Poison damage stacks multiplicatively: 2 poison sources = 3× damage, 3 sources = 6×. A unit with max venom stacks kills anything that survives the initial hit.

### Fortress Discipline (F) Pairs

**F+S — Fortress Stampede**
Elephants adjacent to fortress units get a charge shield: first hit after charging deals 0 damage to the elephant. Charge in, absorb the counter, let the fortress units hold the line.

**F+C — Iron Cavalry**
Cavalry with fortress training can't be displaced by charges or knockback. They hold their ground. Counter-charges against them deal 50% less damage. An immovable mounted wall.

**F+H — Fortress Skirmish**
After hit-and-run retreat, the unit gains a 1-turn "dug in" bonus: +75% defense until next action. They hit, retreat, and fortify. You can't chase them without eating a fortified counterattack.

**F+T — Coastal Fortress**
Naval fortress units project a defense aura onto adjacent land hexes. Land units within 1 hex of a fortress naval unit get +30% defense. Amphibious assaults against fortified coasts are suicide.

**F+N — Citadel**
Units with both fortress and healing create a 2-hex healing aura. All friendly units within 2 hexes heal for 3 HP/turn. The unit itself is immovable (can't be displaced by any ability). The anchor of any army.

**F+J — Hidden Fortress**
Stealth fortress units are invisible until they attack or an enemy enters an adjacent hex. Ambush defense: enemies attacking a stealth fortress unit from surprise get -50% attack for that combat. You never see the wall until you hit it.

**F+A — Desert Stronghold**
Camel fortress units ignore terrain penalties AND project a fortress aura on desert hexes. Desert hexes adjacent to a desert stronghold count as fortified terrain (+30% defense for all friendly units). Turn the desert into a fortress network.

**F+F — Layered Defense**
(Double fortress from multiple sources) Fortress aura stacks: 2 fortress units adjacent to each other create overlapping auras that multiply (+50% each instead of additive +30%). Three fortress units in a triangle = near-impregnable position.

### Stampede Tactics (S) Pairs

**S+C — Thundering Charge**
Cavalry with stampede can charge every turn (no cooldown) and deal knockback on every charge. A cavalry unit that charges, retreats (if also has hit_and_run), and charges again is a non-stop battering ram.

**S+H — Stampede and Scatter**
Elephant charges that knock back enemies also cause them to rout if below 60% HP (up from normal 50% threshold). After knockback, the elephant automatically moves to occupy the vacated hex if empty. Aggressive territory control.

**S+T — Tsunami Charge**
Naval stampede: ships can ram enemy naval units, dealing massive damage and knocking them to an adjacent water hex. If knocked into land, the ship takes double damage. Naval combat becomes violent and positional.

**S+N — Stampeding Growth**
Elephants adjacent to healing units regenerate 5 HP/turn (up from normal healing). Charging elephants heal for the damage they deal. A self-sustaining battering ram.

**S+J — Stealth Stampede**
Elephants with stealth can initiate a charge from stealth (invisible until the charge lands). The charge deals +50% damage from surprise. An invisible elephant suddenly appearing and smashing your line is terrifying.

**S+A — Sandstorm Charge**
Camel stampede: camel units charge through desert terrain dealing AoE damage to all enemies in the charge path (1 hex wide line). Desert charges raise a "sandstorm" that gives -25% accuracy to all enemies within 2 hexes for 1 turn.

**S+S — Stampede Line**
(Double stampede) Multiple stampede units charging in formation: if 2+ stampede units charge from adjacent hexes in the same turn, the knockback becomes a 2-hex push and stuns enemies for 1 turn. Coordinated elephant charges are devastating.

### Cavalry Charge (C) Pairs

**C+H — Endless Charge**
Cavalry with hit-and-run can charge, retreat, and charge again in the same turn if they have enough movement points. A cavalry unit with 3+ moves can charge twice. The definition of cavalry harassment.

**C+T — Wave Riders**
Cavalry that can charge from water onto land (amphibious cavalry). Naval cavalry get +25% charge damage. Beach landings with cavalry hitting before defenders react.

**C+N — Healing Charge**
Cavalry with healing heal all adjacent allies by 2 HP after a charge. A charging healer that restores your line while disrupting the enemy. The paladin archetype.

**C+J — Ghost Cavalry**
Stealth cavalry charge from invisible positions with +75% damage. After the charge, they automatically attempt to re-enter stealth (succeeds if no enemy has line of sight). Strike, vanish, strike again.

**C+A — Desert Cavalry**
Camel cavalry ignore all terrain movement penalties AND can charge through rough terrain (no charge penalty for non-open ground). Desert cavalry charge as effectively on hills and forest as on plains. No terrain is safe.

**C+C — Cavalry Horde**
(Double cavalry) Multiple cavalry units charging in the same turn from adjacent hexes deal +25% damage each. A coordinated cavalry charge is more than the sum of its parts.

### Hit and Run (H) Pairs

**H+T — Coastal Raid**
Naval hit-and-run: after attacking from water, ships retreat to a random adjacent water hex. Coastal raids from the sea — attack, disappear back into the waves. Land units can't retaliate against naval raiders.

**H+N — Healing Retreat**
Units with hit-and-run heal for 3 HP when they retreat. They hit, fall back, and recover. Sustained skirmishers that never need to leave the front line to heal.

**H+J — Shadow Step**
Stealth hit-and-run: after attacking from stealth, the unit automatically re-enters stealth at the retreat hex (no cooldown). Perpetual ambush. The enemy never knows where the next attack comes from.

**H+A — Desert Ghost**
Camel hit-and-run units can retreat through impassable terrain (treat rough terrain as open for retreat movement). You can chase them, but they'll retreat through the mountains you can't follow. They're already gone.

**H+H — Swarm Tactics**
(Double hit-and-run) Units with hit-and-run that retreat to a hex adjacent to another hit-and-run unit gain +1 movement on their next turn. Swarm retreats create a cascading speed advantage. The more skirmishers you have, the faster they all become.

### Tidal Warfare (T) Pairs

**T+N — Tidal Restoration**
Naval units with healing project a 2-hex healing aura from water onto adjacent land. Friendly land units near friendly ships heal 2 HP/turn. Fleet support for land operations.

**T+J — Silent Landing**
Stealth naval units can make amphibious landings without breaking stealth. Enemy coastal defenders don't detect the landing until the stealth units attack. The D-Day nobody saw coming.

**T+A — Desert Fleet**
Camel units adjacent to water/coast hexes gain +25% defense (desert people adapted to water). Naval camel units (if such a thing exists) ignore river/coast movement penalties. Control both desert and water.

**T+T — Armada**
(Double tidal) Multiple naval units in formation (2+ adjacent) project an extended control zone: all enemy naval units within 3 hexes of any ship in the fleet get -20% attack. Fleet dominance through intimidation.

### Nature Healing (N) Pairs

**N+J — Nature's Veil**
Stealth healing units can heal while stealthed without breaking stealth. They're invisible medics. The enemy's units are dying and they don't know why — because the healer they can't see is keeping the assassins alive.

**N+A — Oasis**
Camel units with healing create an "oasis" effect: the hex they occupy and all adjacent hexes count as neutral terrain for movement purposes (no penalties for any unit). A walking oasis that your whole army follows.

**N+N — Life Bloom**
(Double healing) Units with double healing sources create a 3-hex healing aura. All friendly units within 3 hexes heal 4 HP/turn. The unit itself regenerates 6 HP/turn. An army marching with a walking hospital.

### Jungle Stealth (J) Pairs

**J+A — Mirage**
Camel stealth units in desert terrain are permanently stealthed (no cooldown, no action required). Desert + stealth = you literally cannot see them coming. The sand itself hides them.

**J+J — Shadow Network**
(Double stealth) Multiple stealth units within 3 hexes of each other share stealth state — if one is revealed, all others in the network stay hidden. Killing one reveals nothing about the others. Assassination network.

### Polar Summoning (P) Pairs

**P+V — Frostbite Bears**
Summoned bears inflict frostbite: enemies hit by bears take 2 cold damage/turn for 3 turns. Bear attacks slow enemies (1 less move). Frozen and poisoned — the worst combination.

**P+F — Ice Fortress**
Summoned bears adjacent to fortress units gain +50% defense and can't be knocked back. Frozen guardians that hold the line. The bears become living fortifications.

**P+S — Glacial Charge**
Summoned bears can charge (1 hex knockback) if adjacent to an elephant. Bear + elephant combo charges: the bear initiates, the elephant follows up. A two-stage charge assault.

**P+C — Frost Riders**
Summoned bears provide +1 movement to adjacent cavalry. Cavalry near frost bears become faster. Frozen momentum — the cold drives you forward.

**P+H — Frozen Retreat**
When a hit-and-run unit retreats adjacent to a summoned bear, the bear takes the retreat hex's facing position and gains +25% defense for 1 turn. The bear covers the retreat. You hit, your bear fills the gap.

**P+T — Frozen Tide**
Summoned bears on coast/water hexes create ice: the hex becomes difficult terrain for enemies (-1 movement). Naval units near frozen bears move at half speed. Freeze the harbor.

**P+N — Frost Mending**
Summoned bears adjacent to healing units regenerate 4 HP/turn (up from normal 2). Bears become sustainable front-line tanks. Healing + summoning = endless guardians.

**P+J — Frost Wraith**
Stealth units adjacent to summoned bears can "ride" the bear: the stealth unit moves with the bear, maintaining stealth. A stealth assassin on an invisible bear. The bear is the mount, the assassin is the passenger.

**P+A — Sand Bear**
Camel units adjacent to summoned bears share the camel's terrain ignore: bears near camels can move through any terrain without penalty. The bear follows the camel through the mountains. Desert frost — shouldn't exist, but here it is.

**P+P — Polar Legion**
(Double summoning) Summoned bears within 3 hexes of each other gain +25% attack and +25% defense (pack bonus). A bear pack is stronger than individual bears. More bears = exponentially scarier.

### Camel Adaptation (A) Pairs

**A+A — Nomad Network**
(Double camel) Camel units within 3 hexes of each other share terrain ignore: if one camel can traverse a hex type, all camels in the network can. A single camel finding a path through mountains means the whole army follows.

## 120 Triple Stacks

Each faction ends up with exactly 3 domains (1 native + 2 foreign). Every triple gets:
- **All 3 pair synergies** active simultaneously
- **One Emergent bonus** — a unique effect that only exists when all 3 domains are combined

### Emergent Bonus Pattern

The emergent bonus is determined by the *theme intersection* of the 3 domains. Rather than hand-naming 120 abilities, emergent bonuses follow these rules:

**Rule 1: Summoning + Any Combat Domain + Any Mobility Domain = Mount**
If the triple contains P + (V|F|S|C|H|T) + (A|C|H|J):
→ Summoned units inherit the combat domain's effect AND the mobility domain's effect.
→ The bear becomes a `combat_mobility` hybrid. A frost-poison-cavalry bear is a mounted poison bear.

**Rule 2: Healing + Any Defensive Domain + Any Offensive Domain = Paladin/Warrior-Medic**
If the triple contains N + (F|T) + (V|S|C|H):
→ The unit is a self-sustaining combatant: heals for 50% of damage dealt, can't drop below 1 HP from a single hit.

**Rule 3: Stealth + Any Combat Domain + Any Terrain Domain = Terrain Assassin**
If the triple contains J + (V|S|C|H) + (A|T|P):
→ The unit gets permanent stealth on its terrain type. A stealth-stampede-desert unit is permanently invisible on desert and charges from nowhere.

**Rule 4: Fortress + Healing + Any Domain = Anchor**
If the triple contains F + N + (anything):
→ The unit creates a 3-hex zone of control where all friendly units get +30% defense AND heal 3 HP/turn. The unit itself is immovable and regenerates 5 HP/turn. An unkillable anchor.

**Rule 5: Three Mobility Domains = Ghost Army**
If the triple contains 3 of (C, H, A, J):
→ All units in the faction ignore all terrain penalties AND gain +1 movement. The entire army is a ghost that goes wherever it wants.

**Rule 6: Three Combat Domains = Juggernaut**
If the triple contains 3 of (V, S, C, F):
→ All combat bonuses are doubled. Stampede, charge, poison, fortress — everything hits twice as hard. Pure destruction.

**Rule 7: Otherwise = Adaptive**
Any triple not matching rules 1-6 gets a generic emergent bonus: all pair synergy effects are multiplied by 1.5×. Good, but not legendary.

## Implementation Notes

### Single-Domain Abilities (Base Effects)
Each domain has a base passive effect that works without any synergies:
- **V**: Melee attacks apply 1 poison stack (2 dmg/turn, 3 turns)
- **F**: Adjacent friendly units get +30% defense
- **S**: Charge attacks knock back 1 hex
- **C**: Charge attacks strike first; if enemy dies, attacker takes 0 damage
- **H**: After combat, unit retreats to safest adjacent hex
- **T**: Naval units can attack from water to land; enemies on coast get -25% defense
- **N**: Unit heals 2 HP/turn; adjacent allies heal 1 HP/turn
- **J**: Unit is stealthed; first attack from stealth deals +50% damage
- **P**: Can summon 1 frost bear per 5 turns; bears have 20 HP, 5 atk, 3 def
- **A**: Unit ignores all terrain movement penalties

### Pair Synergy Activation
A pair synergy activates when a unit's prototype has tags matching BOTH domains. The synergy modifies combat/movement/healing calculations.

### Triple Stack Activation
A triple stack activates when a faction has learned all 3 domains. ALL units of that faction gain the 3 pair synergies AND the emergent bonus. This makes the faction's entire army transform at end-game.

### Balance Approach
These abilities are intentionally overpowered. Fun > Optuna score. The balance lever is:
- **Knowledge acquisition speed** — how many turns of exposure before you learn a domain
- **Prototype cost scaling** — first foreign prototype is 2× cost
- **Domain cap** — max 3, first-come-first-served
- **Emergent rules** — the 7 rules above can be tuned individually
